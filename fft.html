We have \[\frac{1}{A}=\int_{0}^{\infty}{\rm e}^{-\alpha A}d\alpha\] and \[\frac{1}{AB}=\int_{0}^{\infty}d\alpha d\beta{\rm e}^{-\alpha A-\beta B}\] Next we compute the Fourier transform of the pathological function \[f(x,y)=\frac{1}{(x+{\rm i}0^{+})(x^{2}+y^{2}-a^{2})}\] which is

\[\begin{aligned} f(k_{1},k_{2}) & = & \int dxdy\frac{{\rm e}^{-{\rm i}(k_{1}x+k_{2}y)}}{(x+0^{+})(x^{2}+y^{2}-a^{2})}\\ & = & \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}dxdy\int_{0}^{\infty}dsdt\exp\left\{ -{\rm i}k_{1}x-{\rm i}k_{2}y-s(x+{\rm i}0^{+})-t(x^{2}+y^{2}-a^{2})\right\} \end{aligned}\]

Next, letâ€™s look at the exponent,

\[\begin{aligned} E & = & -t\left(x^{2}+\frac{s+{\rm i}k_{1}}{t}x\right)-t\left(y^{2}+\frac{{\rm i}k_{2}}{t}y\right)+ta^{2}-{\rm i}0^{+}s\\ & = & -t\left[\left(x+\frac{s+{\rm i}k_{1}}{2t}\right)^{2}-\left(\frac{s+{\rm i}k_{1}}{t}\right)^{2}\right]-t\left[\left(t-\frac{{\rm i}k_{2}}{2t}y\right)^{2}-\left(\frac{{\rm i}k_{2}}{2t}\right)^{2}\right]+ta^{2}-{\rm i}0^{+}s\end{aligned}\]

Let \[x'=t^{1/2}\left(x+\frac{s+{\rm i}k_{1}}{2t}\right)\] \[y'=t^{1/2}\left(t-\frac{{\rm i}k_{2}}{2t}y\right)\] Then

\[\begin{aligned} f(\mathbf{k}) & = & \int_{0}^{\infty}\frac{\pi}{t}\exp\left\{ \frac{(s+{\rm i}k_{1})^{2}-k_{2}^{2}}{t}+a^{2}t-{\rm i}0^{+}s\right\} dsdt\\ & = & \pi\int_{0}^{\infty}\left\{ \int_{0}^{\infty}\frac{dt}{t}\exp\left(\frac{s^{2}-\mathbf{k}^{2}+2{\rm i}sk_{1}}{t}+a^{2}t-{\rm i}0^{+}s\right)\right\} ds\end{aligned}\]

It appears to be the modified Bessel function but at \(t=0\) the integrand is finite only for \(s^{2}<\mathbf{k}^{2}\) where the essential singularity kills the pole while at large \(t\) the integrand diverges exponentially. Some regulator should be added (shifting \(x\) to \(x+{\rm i}0^{+}\) does not help) according to the physical situation where the pathology occurred.
